🎯 The 8 Golden Rules

🎯

Start Simple

1 working feature > 10 broken features. Complexity kills projects.

💾

Commit Often

Git save EVERY hour. When laptop dies at 3 AM, you'll thank yourself.

😴

Sleep 4+ Hours

Tired brain codes badly. Quick nap > 5 energy drinks.

🤝

Help Others

Talk to other teams. Steal ideas. Share solutions. Make friends!

⏰

Time Buffer

Leave 2 hours for bugs and presentation. Demos ALWAYS break.

😊

Smile in Demo

Even if everything breaks, present confidently. Judges love effort!

📸

Screenshots

Take screenshots throughout. If demo fails, you have backup!

🔍

Google Everything

90% of problems solved on Stack Overflow. Copy smart, not hard!

🚨 When Everything Breaks (2 Hours Before Deadline)

Emergency Recovery Protocol:

1. BREATHE

Panicking wastes time. You got this! 💪

2. RESTORE git log
git checkout [old-commit]

Go back to when it worked

3. SIMPLIFY

Show what DOES work. Cut broken features!

4. BE HONEST

"We faced X, learned Y" - Judges respect honesty!

🎤 Presentation Pro Tips

The Perfect 3-Minute Pitch:

0:00-0:30 - The Hook

"Imagine you lost your laptop on campus. Where do you report it? Nowhere! That's the problem we solved."

0:30-1:30 - The Solution

"Meet FindIt - a Lost & Found portal. Students post lost items, others can claim. Simple, effective."

1:30-2:30 - The Demo

"Let me show you... [Click through main feature]. As you can see, it takes 30 seconds to post an item."

2:30-3:00 - The Close

"We built this in 10 hours using HTML/CSS/JS. With more time, we'd add notifications. Thank you!"

🎯 Presentation Hacks:
  • Practice on strangers: Grab random people, pitch to them
  • Record yourself: Watch it back, fix awkward parts
  • Have backup plan: If demo breaks, show screenshots/video
  • Smile & make eye contact: Confidence > Perfection
  • Prepare for "Why this?": Have a personal story
